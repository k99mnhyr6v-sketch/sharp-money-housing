# Sharp Money Housing

**The list price is the public line. This tool helps you find out if it's worth taking.**

A working tool for homebuyers that runs four independent, fact-based counter-arguments against an asking price — the kind of analysis a buyer's agent's commission structure rarely volunteers.

🔗 **Live tool:** _add your GitHub Pages URL here once you have it_

## What it does

Four exhibits, each switchable on/off depending on whether it fits the property:

- **A — Rate-Adjusted Payment Reality.** Compares what the seller actually pays monthly (at their purchase price and original financing rate) against what you'd pay monthly at the list price and today's rate — plus a benchmark against the national Case-Shiller home price index, so "appreciation" claims have to clear a real bar.
- **B — Comp De-Rating.** Adjusts nearby comparable sales for square footage and for the interest-rate environment they sold in, so a 2022 comp at 3% financing doesn't get treated as equivalent to a 2026 purchase at today's rates.
- **C — The Seller's Own Rent Test.** If the property was ever listed for rent, compares that asking rent against the full monthly carrying cost of owning it at the list price.
- **D — Embedded Wear & Deferred Capital.** Estimates consumed remaining-useful-life on big-ticket components (roof, HVAC, flooring, etc.), scaled to the property's actual square footage, with an optional adjustment for likely pet-related flooring wear.

Every dollar figure includes a low–high range, not just a point estimate, since the underlying assumptions (historical rate lookups, $/sqft costs, comp normalization) carry real uncertainty.

## What it's not

This is an informational tool, not legal, financial, or fiduciary advice. It doesn't recommend an offer price or a negotiation strategy — it surfaces facts and lets you decide what to do with them. Always loop in your own agent, attorney, lender, and inspector.

## How to use it

1. Open the live link above
2. Fill in the property's list price and whatever details apply per exhibit
3. Switch off any exhibit that doesn't fit the property (e.g. skip Exhibit C if it was never rent-listed)
4. Use "Generate Exportable Summary" to get a plain-text copy of your numbers to save or share
