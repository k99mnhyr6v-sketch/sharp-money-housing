B Comp De-Rating: Adjusting Neighborhood Comps for Size and the Rate Environment They Sold In A smaller comp that sold at 3% tells you even less than it looks like — two adjustments, applied separately Use this when the subject property last sold long ago (so Exhibit B doesn't apply directly) but the seller/agent is anchoring price off neighborhood comps from 2020–2023. Each comp gets adjusted in two separate steps so you can see how much of the gap is size and how much is rate: (1) normalize to the subject's square footage via $/sqft, (2) re-price that size-normalized figure at today's rate, holding monthly payment constant. Add a row per comp. Limitation, stated plainly: $/sqft is a blunt normalization. It doesn't capture lot size, finish quality, layout, or condition — two houses can be the same square footage and not be comparable. Use this to size-correct the obvious mismatch, not as a precise appraisal substitute. If a comp's size is within ~10% of the subject, the size adjustment will be small and the rate adjustment is doing most of the work; if it's a real mismatch (1,800 vs 2,800 sqft), the size step matters a lot and is worth a sentence of explanation if you cite it.
